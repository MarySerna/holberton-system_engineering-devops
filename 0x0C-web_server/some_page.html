History

Antioquia is one of the thirty-two departments that make up the Republic of Colombia. Its capital is Medellín, the second most populated city in the country. It is locat\ ed in the northwest of the country, in the Andean and Caribbean regions, bounded on the north by the Caribbean Sea (Atlantic Ocean), Córdoba and Bolívar, on the east by Santander a\ nd Boyacá, on the south by Caldas and Risaralda, and on the west by Chocó. With about 6.5 million inhabitants. in 2015 it is the most populated department and with 63 600 km², the \ sixth largest, surpassed by Amazonas, Vichada, Caquetá, Meta and Guainía...

Geography

The territory of the Department of Antioquia is characterized by a varied relief, represented, on the one hand, by flat areas that comprise two thirds of the total land,\ and which are located in the Magdalena Valley, in the sub-region known as Bajo Cauca, in the areas near the department of Chocó, and in the areas near the coastal region of Urabá;\ and on the other hand, the relief is conformed by an extensive very mountainous area that is part of the Central and Western Cordilleras of the Andes, which make up a third of Ant\ ioquia's soil, and in which 202 important highs stand out, with elevations that oscillate between 1,000 and 4,080 meters above sea level...

Food

A very distinctive feature of the Antioquia region is its gastronomy. The main food base is corn. The arepa is the most important food product of corn, since it serves a\ s a complement for all meals. There are diverse arepas: arepa de chócolo, thin arepa, round arepa, arepa de monte or peeled corn, among others. The mazamorra is another food produc\ t of corn, like empanadas, which are of different kinds. The sancocho paisa is typical for lunch, the same as the dish of beans. However, the most representative dish is the Paisa tray, or mountaineer dish, which consists of wh\ ite rice, cargamanto beans (red), powdered meat, pork rinds, sausage or blood sausage, fried egg, avocado, ripe banana slices, patacón and arepa; It can also be accompanied with re\ d sliced hogao and tomato. As a dessert, it is customary to take the mazamorra of corn with panela tiruda or scraped, although the accompaniment has been extended to sodas or dri\ nks such as guarapo, which is sugarcane juice with lemon. At Christmas the custard, the fritters and the corn flakes are used. Other typical dishes are tamales and mondongo soup.
